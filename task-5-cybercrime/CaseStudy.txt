Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trustworthy source. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that look genuine.

A typical phishing attack happens step-by-step. First, the attacker creates a fake message that appears to come from a bank, online service, or company. Next, the message urges the victim to take immediate action, such as clicking a link or verifying their account. Then, the victim is redirected to a fake website that looks real. When the victim enters their personal details, the attacker collects the information and uses it for fraud.

Phishing commonly targets students, working professionals, and elderly people who may not be aware of such scams. People who frequently use online banking or social media are also at higher risk.

The consequences of phishing can be serious. Victims may lose money from their bank accounts, have their personal data stolen, or face identity theft. In some cases, their social media or email accounts may also be hacked.

To prevent phishing, individuals should avoid clicking on unknown links, verify the sender, and never share sensitive information online without proper confirmation. Awareness and caution are the best defenses against such cybercrimes.
